###### A.1.10.2.4.3.1.3.1 - Core Facilitator Final Inclusion Decision [Core]

Where consensus cannot be reached on whether to include an item in the Spell, the disagreement must be raised in the govops channel in Slack so that the Spell Teams, Core GovOps, the Core Facilitator, and the Protocol Security Workstream Lead have visibility into the disagreement and the reasoning behind it. The Core Facilitator must hear all positions and make the final decision on whether to include the item. The Core Facilitator's decision is binding for the Executive Vote in question.

Routine coordination among the Spell Teams in their own working channels is expected. A disagreement over whether to include an item must not, however, be resolved in a way that excludes the Core Facilitator or that settles the matter privately and reports only the outcome. The positions of each party, and the reasoning through which the disagreement is resolved, must be visible in the govops channel in Slack.

Where a security or confidentiality concern makes it necessary to discuss such a disagreement in a private channel, the parties to that discussion must afterwards provide a substantive accounting in the govops channel in Slack, setting out the positions of each party and how the settled position was reached.

Where there is credible suspicion that the Core Facilitator is compromised, Core GovOps makes the final inclusion decision for the Executive Vote in question in the Core Facilitator's place, and the adjudication process specified in [A.1.7.6 - Acting Against Misalignment](7aafa61e-8649-41fb-8c3f-64e5714f9f18) applies.
